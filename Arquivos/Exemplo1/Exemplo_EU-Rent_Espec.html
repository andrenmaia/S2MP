Violated Quality Item(s)

A set of Definitions must not be circular
Description

Circular sets of Definitions literally express meanings that can never be pinned down (resolved) precisely. Left unclear is whether two or more things are the same, or if not, how exactly they differ. A circular set of Definitions is generally of the form:

  • "an X is a Y that ...", "a Y is an X that ...",
  • "an X is a Y that ...", "a Y is a Z that ...", "a Z is an X that ...".
  • ... and so on.
Motivation

To ensure that all Definitions lead to meaningful distinctions.
Examples

Improper example:

  • Definition of Product: an item needed by a party
  • Definition of Item: a product ordered by a customer

Proper:

  • Definition of Product: a good produced for sale
  • Definition of Item: a good ordered by a customer
  • Definition of Good: (as per dictionary definition)

Concept must be defined, computed or derived
Description

Every base Term (one that is not derived or computed) must have an explicit Definition to convey its meaning. (In straightforward cases, the Definition can be based directly on a dictionary definition.) Every derived or computed Term must have a Rule indicating the logic for its derivation or computation, a Definition, or optionally, both.
Motivation

To ensure the meaning of a term has been made explicit.
To avoid misinterpretation.
Examples

Example term: Salary

Definition: Salary is ...
Computation: Salary must be computed as ... Derivation: Salary is a [other known term] that ...
